Mount Fuji

Japan’s highest volcano (3,776m), iconic snow‑capped cone, UNESCO cultural heritage, sacred pilgrimage site, inspired centuries of art and worship.

Amazon Rainforest

Vast tropical expanse across nine countries, richest biodiversity, massive carbon sink, vital climate regulator, home to millions and Indigenous cultures.

The Great Barrier Reef

The Great Barrier Reef is the world's largest coral reef system, rich in marine biodiversity, located off Australia's northeastern coast.

Sahara Desert

Harsh but majestic: Africa’s vast Sahara spans ~3.6Mmi², with dunes, plateaus, oases—extreme heat by day, cold nights

Seven Sisters Waterfall

Seven elegant streams cascade drop ~ 410m into Geirangerfjord, tallest drop ~250m. Stunning UNESCO fjord beauty, boat best viewpoint.

The Himalayas

Abode of snow: Himalayas stretch ~2,500 km across five nations, host world’s highest peaks, glaciers, rivers; still rising, shaping climate, culture.
